Mission Briefing

Humanity faces an uncertain future on Earth. Our mission is to explore distant worlds and discover a new home beyond the stars — before time runs out.

Primary Objective

Traverse the wormhole near Saturn and evaluate three candidate planets in the Gargantua star system. Collect atmospheric, geological, and biological data. Identify one viable candidate for humanity's second home. Return with findings or transmit data via relay beacon before orbital decay.

Ship Systems

  • Endurance-class vessel — 12 cryosleep pods, 2 AI cores
  • Ranger shuttles × 2 for planetary descent
  • Nuclear thermal propulsion — Δv: 340 km/s
  • Gravitational anomaly scanner (TARS-IV)
  • Quantum relay communications array

Mission Timeline

  • Year 1 — Transit to Saturn wormhole entry point
  • Year 2 — Wormhole transit · Approach Gargantua
  • Year 3–5 — Planet evaluation: Miller, Edmunds, Mann
  • Year 6 — Data transmission / Return burn
  • Year 10 — Earth arrival (accounting for time dilation)
